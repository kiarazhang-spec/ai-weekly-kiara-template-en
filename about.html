ABOUT

Hey, I'm Kiara.

Here's why this template exists and how to use it.
I'm a product manager in the Bay Area. I run a Chinese-language AI weekly at kiara-weekly-ai, and I built this English edition as a companion template — a fully working scaffold plus one real example issue plus a complete editorial rulebook, so anyone can fork it and run their own weekly without reinventing the plumbing or the voice. This is not an active newsletter I publish every week; it's a template I made public because the friction to start one is much higher than it should be.

👋 Who this is for

  • PMs, analysts, and researchers who want to write their own weekly AI signal-sheet and need a working starting point
  • People who tried to start a newsletter and got stuck on infrastructure, editorial voice, or "what's my source hierarchy actually"
  • Anyone forking to learn — the July 6, 2026 example issue is a real synthesis from real English primary sources, not lorem ipsum

📦 What's in the box

  • A working static site — homepage, archive, per-issue pages, mobile-responsive, deploys to GitHub Pages for $0
  • One fully worked example issue (week of July 6, 2026) showing what the finished output looks like
  • A complete editorial rulebook — AGENTS.md — covering voice, structure, source hierarchy, fair-use protocol, and a specific carve-out for China-native model labs
  • Per-story "Read" callouts and weekly "Pattern" synthesis blocks, not generic Angle / Take / Hot Takes

📚 How I pick what to cover

Sources are tiered by evidence type, not by how much any one outlet is trusted:

  • Primary sources — company blogs and model cards, SEC filings, arXiv papers. First-hand material; arXiv results are flagged as pre-print until independently replicated
  • Investigative reporting — The Information, Bloomberg, Reuters, FT, WSJ. Original reporting with named sources; leaned on for anything not verifiable from a filing
  • Independent analysis — Stratechery, Latent Space, Semianalysis, Import AI, Interconnects, Platformer. Cited as informed opinion, not fact
  • Context only — trade press, aggregators, VC memos. Useful for gauging attention; never the sole citation
  • Chinese-language sources — out of scope, with one exception: for China-native model labs (Moonshot, DeepSeek, Qwen, Zhipu), first-hand Chinese-only claims are translated and flagged

🚫 What this isn't

  • Not an active newsletter. There's one worked example issue. I'm not planning to publish weekly here — the point is to hand you the scaffold
  • Not investment advice. The Read and Pattern sections in the example issue are how I read the market at the time, not what you should do with money
  • Not a translation of my Chinese newsletter. Different sources, different audience, different editorial voice by design
  • Not opinionated on tooling. Write issues by hand, with an AI assistant, or however you want — the rulebook is voice- and source-focused, not workflow-prescriptive

🔗 Attribution & fair use

The example issue and any issue produced from this template should link every claim to its source. Quotes stay short — a few sentences at most — and always wrapped in the author's own commentary. The goal is to send readers toward the original, not to replace it.

  • Facts (numbers, dates, events) are rewritten in the author's own words and linked back
  • Analytical frameworks are attributed to whoever came up with them
  • No images from paywalled outlets. Charts from papers or company blogs are captioned with the source
  • If you're the author of something cited in the example issue and want a correction, better link, or shorter excerpt, file an issue

🍴 Fork it

  • Click "Use this template" on the GitHub repo or clone directly
  • Search-and-replace Kiara / kiarazhang-spec / repo URLs across index.html, about.html, README.md, and the example post footer
  • Change --green in assets/style.css to recolor the site
  • Rewrite the masthead and About page in your voice
  • Enable Pages: Settings → Pages → Source: GitHub Actions
  • Write your first issue as posts/YYYY-MM-DD.html, add it to manifest.json, push

If you fork and publish, a link back to this template somewhere in your footer or About is appreciated but not required — MIT for code, CC BY 4.0 for the example issue content.
