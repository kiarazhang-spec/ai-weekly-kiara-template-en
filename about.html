ABOUT

Hey, I'm Kiara.

Here's why this exists and who it's for.
I'm a product manager in the Bay Area. My job — and my hobby, honestly — is figuring out what's actually happening in AI vs. what people are performing on social media. Every week I read a few hundred sources, throw most of it away, and write up the stuff that changed how I think. This is that.

👋 Who this is for

  • PMs building AI features who need to know what shipped last week without spending three hours on it
  • Founders and operators trying to distinguish real capability shifts from marketing cycles
  • Investors and researchers who want the primary-source trail already assembled
  • Basically: things shipped, not things posted

🎯 What you'll get

  • Every Monday, 8am Pacific — one issue covering the previous week (Mon–Sun PT)
  • Top 5 stories with links to the actual source (not a repost of a repost)
  • My honest read on what each story means for people building product — labeled "Read"
  • Three patterns tying the week together — labeled "Pattern"

📚 How I pick what to cover

I read primary sources first — company blogs, SEC filings, arXiv papers — then investigative reporting (The Information, Bloomberg, Reuters, FT, WSJ) and sharp independent analysts (Stratechery, Latent Space, Semianalysis, Import AI). Trade press and aggregators are context only, never the sole citation.

One exception: for China-native model labs (Moonshot, DeepSeek, Qwen, Zhipu), I'll translate first-hand Chinese-only claims and flag the translation explicitly.

🚫 What this isn't

  • Not a news feed. If you want breaking news, X is faster. This is the "what did we learn this week" digest
  • Not investment advice. The Read and Pattern sections are how I see the market, not what you should do with money
  • Not my employer's position. Everything here is personal opinion on public information
  • Not comprehensive. I miss things. If I miss something important, tell me

🔗 Attribution & fair use

Every claim in this newsletter links to its source. Quotes are kept short — a few sentences at most — and always wrapped in my own commentary. The goal is to send you toward the original, not to replace it.

  • Facts (numbers, dates, events) are rewritten in my own words and linked back
  • Analytical frameworks are attributed to the person who came up with them
  • No images from paywalled outlets. Charts from papers or company blogs are captioned with the source
  • If you're the author of something I've cited and you'd like a correction, better link, or the excerpt shortened, file an issue or email me — I fix quickly

✉️ Talk to me

  • Spotted a factual error? File it on GitHub Issues with the primary source. I fix quickly and note the change
  • Have a take I should hear? DM me — I read everything, reply to most
  • Want to republish, cite, or syndicate? Yes to attribution, usually yes to more. Reach out
  • Chinese speaker? There's a Chinese sister edition — separate content, not a translation
