ABOUT

Hey, I'm Kiara.

Here's why this exists and who it's for.
I'm a product manager in the Bay Area. My job — and my hobby, honestly — is figuring out what's actually happening in AI vs. what people are performing on Twitter. Every week I read a few hundred sources, throw most of it away, and write up the stuff that changed how I think. This is that.

👋 Who this is for

  • PMs building AI features who need to know what shipped last week without spending three hours on it
  • Founders and operators trying to distinguish real capability shifts from marketing cycles
  • Investors and researchers who want the primary-source trail already assembled
  • Basically: people who ship things, not people who tweet things

🎯 What you'll get

  • Every Monday, 8am Pacific — one issue covering the previous week (Mon–Sun PT)
  • Top 5 stories with links to the actual source (not a repost of a repost)
  • My honest read on what each story means for people building product — labeled "Angle"
  • Three themes tying the week together — labeled "Take"
  • Things I'm watching next week, so you're not surprised on Monday

📚 How I pick what to cover

Sources are tiered. I click through everything myself before it goes in — no repost-of-a-repost chains.

  • Top of the stack: the companies themselves — OpenAI, Anthropic, DeepMind, Meta AI, Mistral, xAI, Hugging Face blogs; earnings calls; SEC filings; arXiv papers
  • Trusted second read: Stratechery, The Information, Latent Space, Semianalysis, Import AI, Interconnects, Platformer, plus Reuters / Bloomberg / FT / WSJ for verification
  • Context only: trade press and analyst pieces — helpful, but never the only source for a claim
  • Chinese-language sources: normally out of scope, with one exception for China-native model labs (Moonshot, DeepSeek, Qwen, etc.) where the first-hand info is Chinese-only. In those cases I translate the specific claim and label it clearly

🚫 What this isn't

  • Not a news feed. If you want breaking news, X is faster. This is the "what did we learn this week" digest
  • Not investment advice. The Angle and Take sections are how I read the market, not what you should do with money
  • Not my employer's position. Everything here is personal opinion on public information
  • Not comprehensive. I miss things. If I miss something important, tell me

🤖 How AI fits into this

Look — I write about AI. It would be weird to pretend I don't use it. Here's the honest breakdown of what's me and what's the machines:

  • What the AI does: helps me sweep through a few hundred sources every week (I'd never get through them alone), pulls quotes and dates out of dense filings, suggests headline options I usually rewrite, catches typos and awkward sentences on the final pass
  • What I do: pick every story that makes the cut, click through every primary source myself, write every "Angle" and "Take" — the opinion is mine and it's the whole point, decide when something is signal vs. noise, own every mistake
  • What I never do: publish an AI-generated take as if it were mine, cite a source I haven't opened, use AI to invent numbers or quotes, ship a draft I haven't rewritten in my own voice

The test I use: if I removed the AI tools tomorrow, this newsletter would be slower and cover fewer sources, but it would still sound like me and hold the same opinions. That's the line. AI is a research assistant with a very fast reading speed. It's not the writer.

If a specific issue leans harder on AI than usual — say, I used it to parse a 200-page filing or draft a first pass I heavily rewrote — I'll note it in that issue's footer. Over-disclosure over under-disclosure, always.
